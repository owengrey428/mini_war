Mid-Career Recruiting

The Volunteer-to-Staff Pathway

ATHLETES
IN ACTION

Recruiting mid-career professionals is a highly relational, two-way evaluation process. We invite marketplace professionals to experience Victory Beyond Competition by utilizing their established skills to build spiritual movements and multiply disciples through sport.

1. Evaluate Missional Gaps

Audit your 20 square feet. Identify areas ripe for volunteer ownership in Strategic Operations, Relational Mentorship, or Pioneering Missional Gaps to create roles with true Kingdom impact.

3. Match Volunteer Types

Empower them to grow their buy-in over time:

  • Hospitality: Event-based support.
  • Faithful: Specialized skills in local ministry.
  • Affiliate: High commitment; functions like part-time staff.

5 & 6. Transition & MPD

Make "The Ask" based on their track record of fruitfulness. Frame it as the natural next step.

Flourishing Staff Focus: Connect them with peers to normalize Ministry Partner Development. Offer part-time "tent-making" flexibility to ease the transition.

2. Sourcing & The Invitation

Cultivate oneness by casting a vision for vocational significance.

Where to Look:

Donors, local church small groups, AIA Alumni, Christian coaches, and community networks.

The High-Impact Pitch:

Mid-career professionals don't just want to be extra hands; they want to utilize their marketplace skills for Kingdom value.

"I see the leadership skills God gave you. You could make a massive eternal impact by utilizing those talents with our team to help us build a spiritual movement here."

4. Relational Shepherding

Honor their life experience. AIA must be a place where they serve AND are developed.

Custom Equipping

Assess gaps relationally in 4 areas: Theological, Relational, Intellectual, & Operational. Avoid rigid corporate training.

Consistent Exposure

Maintain regular touchpoints outside of volunteer hours to build deep trust and mutual growth.

Focus on Multiplication

Position volunteers to directly facilitate life change. Once they experience the joy of seeing an athlete they mentored begin to multiply their faith into others, the vision is caught organically.

A Christ-follower on every team, in every sport, in every nation.
